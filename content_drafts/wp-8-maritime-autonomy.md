## 2. Technical Deep-Dive: The SAP-2026 Tiers

The 2026 standard defines three levels of maritime autonomy:

### Tier 1: Decision Support (Al-Augmented)

The crew is on board, but the AI manages collision avoidance and fuel optimization. The human is the "Safety Pilot."

### Tier 2: Remotely Controlled (Human in the Loop)

The ship is crewless, but monitored 24/7 by a Master Mariner in a land-based **Remote Control Center (RCC)** via satellite link. One "Shor-Captain" can manage up to five vessels simultaneously.

### Tier 3: Fully Autonomous (Human on the Loop)

The vessel makes all decisions independently. Human intervention only occurs in "Unbound Scenarios" (unpredicted storms or mechanical failure) via a high-priority "Emergency Uplink."

### 3.1. Optical-LIDAR Fusion

Autonomous ships in 2026 don't just use Radar. They use a fusion of LIDAR and multi-spectral cameras to "see" smaller obstacles (fishing boats, plastic debris) that Radar misses.
