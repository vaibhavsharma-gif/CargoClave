Ground-based visibility is no longer sufficient. This whitepaper analyzes the integration of high-frequency satellite imagery, RF-tracking, and orbital AI with standard logistics platforms. Historically, a ship in the middle of the Pacific was a "Black Box." AIS (Automatic Identification System) was limited by terrestrial range and prone to tampering.

In 2026, we have achieved **Continuous Global Observation**. With over 5,000 small satellites dedicated to trade monitoring, no container is invisible for more than 5 minutes.

## 2. Technical Deep-Dive: The Orbital Infrastructure

Unlike optical cameras, SAR can see through clouds, smoke, and darkness.

- **2026 Use-Case:** Detecting "Dark Vessels" (ships that have turned off their transponders) by matching their SAR signature against the global registry.

L-LEO satellites in 2026 can measure the "Live Carbon Exhaust" of a vessel or individual truck.

- **Impact:** This data is used to verify "Green Freight" claims in real-time, preventing "Greenwashing" in Scope 3 reporting.

### 2.3. Orbital AI (Edge-in-Space)

Satellites in 2026 don't just send raw video down. They have onboard chips that run "Port-Congestion Models."

- **Efficiency:** Instead of sending gigabytes of image data, they send a single number: "Port of Long Beach - Congestion Index 8.4 - Expected Delay +12h."
